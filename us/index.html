Case Study
Hybrid LLM Brand Classification
A production AI decisioning product in Amazon Ads where the challenge was not "can an LLM do this?" but "what architecture and evidence model make this safe and valuable enough to ship?"
The Product Problem

Query-brand relationship quality affected ad relevance, CTR, and monetization. The real challenge was improving semantic decision quality in a production auction environment without creating unacceptable latency, reliability, or cost risk.

My Role

I helped frame the work as an AI decisioning product, clarified output boundaries, shaped the architecture tradeoff, and helped define the launch metrics and rollout logic. The value I added here was PM-shaped judgment: what should ship first, what risk was acceptable, and what evidence made rollout credible.

Product Judgment

We avoided a real-time LLM-first path for v1. The product used bounded outputs, structured context, offline or near-offline classification, Redis serving, and a legacy fallback path.

Evaluation

50,000-query golden dataset, label rubric, hallucination checks, schema validation, shadow auditing, and monitoring for latency, cache hit, fallback rate, and output distribution.

Outcome

  • about 99% offline accuracy
  • about 22% lower latency
  • about $130M business impact

What this shows

  • problem framing under ambiguity
  • architecture tradeoff judgment
  • evaluation design
  • launch guardrails and business orientation
Case Study
Churn Prediction Service
A productized retention signal for SMB advertisers where the hardest part was not modeling churn but making the output usable, trustworthy, and operationally consumable by real teams.
The Product Problem

Downstream teams needed earlier and more actionable signals to intervene before advertiser value was lost. Prediction alone was not enough; the workflow had to support action.

My Role

I framed the work as a lifecycle warning product, clarified the target users, and made the metric logic explicit: recall mattered more than precision because false negatives were costlier. This is the kind of product judgment I want teams to associate with me.

Operationalization

The capability moved toward stable APIs, artifact bundling, CI/CD-backed deployment, and service-level monitoring so downstream teams could actually trust and adopt it.

PM Signal

This case shows service thinking, workflow adoption, metric tradeoff judgment, and end-to-end ownership from model output to business consumption.

Outcome

  • recall from 81.8% to 92.8%
  • integration from about 1 week to about 10 minutes
  • deployment from days to about 15 minutes

What this shows

  • cross-functional usability focus

Artifacts
What I would bring to the role beyond narrative.
A strong AI PM portfolio should not stop at storytelling. The practical signal is whether the candidate knows how to turn ambiguity into product artifacts, evaluation criteria, and launch gates that other teams can actually execute against.

Sample Product Artifacts I Know How to Define

  • Problem framing: target user, workflow bottleneck, business cost of failure, and why AI is or is not the right approach
  • Output boundary: tightly scoped output schemas instead of open-ended model behavior
  • Success criteria: offline quality bar, online quality bar, and business metric alignment
  • Rollout plan: shadow mode, phased launch, rollback conditions, and post-launch review cadence

Evaluation and Launch Readiness Thinking

  • Eval stack: golden datasets, failure taxonomy, hallucination checks, schema validation, and online monitoring
  • Tradeoff logic: explicit decisions across quality, latency, cost, reliability, and adoption
  • Workflow adoption: who consumes the output, what action they take next, and what happens when confidence is low
  • Operational guardrails: fallback systems, human review points, observability, and launch thresholds
